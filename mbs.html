Artist Statement

I created this project, as it not only was a task, but to learn more about myself as well. This project is a metaphorical reflection of who I am in my mind, body, and spirit. I have connected my mind, body, and spirit through connecting words and images that symbolize each individually and altogether as a whole. I started the project out by placing the images I collected and created a layout for each section of the overall project. I wanted to give my project an exciting effect. Each part has multiple bright elements to it and I believe that each stands out greatly on their own and as a whole. I wanted my audience to have this “wow” effect when viewing my work because it is how I want to be viewed as a person. It was difficult to create this effect at first, as many of the images I chose were bland and boring on their own, as well with the rest of the images in the first layouts. Overall, my project came out essentially how I imagined it, which is not only important professionally, but personally as well. This seems to be a pattern in just about all of my work; where I am unsure at first, but it eventually comes out the way I planned. I may overthink throughout my entire process, but I believe it helps in the end. This way, I am more focused on the task at hand. This was also useful in the creation of my project, as I wanted this image or that image, but who is to say I cannot have both? My overthinking has helped to create all of the elements within my project essentially. I can only hope that my future work will be as spontaneous as this project project was. I have learned many valuable things with the creation of this project. I have gained further knowledge in the art world, such as fundamentals of basic art and of the programs I used. I have also learned more about myself as a person and more about my preference of art and art style. Figuring out my art process was a major accomplishment as well. I have overall enjoyed this project as whole, and hope to create many more pieces alike.
